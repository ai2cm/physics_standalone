12 !! - The parameterization of stationary convectively-forced GWD follows
13 !! the development of Chun and Baik (1998) \cite chun_and_baik_1998 ,
14 !! which was tested in GCMs by Chun et al. (2001,2004)
15 !! \cite chun_et_al_2001 \cite chun_et_al_2004 was implemented in GFS
16 !! by Ake Johansson (2008) and the work of the GCWMB staff. Modest
17 !! positive effects from using the parameterization are seen in the
18 !! tropical upper troposphere and lower stratosphere.
19 !!
20 !> Parameterizing subgrid-scale convection-induced gravity wave
21 !! momentum flux for use in large-scale models inherently requires
22 !! some information from subgrid-scale cumulus parameterization.
23 !! The methodology for parameterizing the zonal momentum flux induced
24 !! by thermal forcing can be summarized as follows. From the cloud-base
25 !! to cloud-top height, the effect of the momentum flux
26 !! induced by subgrid-scale diabatic forcing is not considered because
27 !! subgrid-scale cumulus convection in large-scale models is only
28 !! activated in a conditionally unstable atmosphere. Below the cloud
29 !! base, the momentum flux is also not considered because of the wave
30 !! momentum cancellation. At the cloud top, the momentum flux is
31 !! obtained by eq.(18) and (19) in Chun and Baik (1998)
32 !! \cite chun_and_baik_1998. Above the cloud top, there are two ways to
33 !! construct the momentum flux profile. One way is to specify a
34 !! vertical structure of the momentum flux normalized by the cloud-top
35 !! value, similar to what has been done for mountain drag
36 !! parameterization. The other way is to apply the wave saturation
37 !! hypothesis in order to find wave breaking levels in terms of the
38 !! Richardon number criterion using the nonlinearity factor of
39 !! thermally induced waves.
